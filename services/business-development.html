Operational, not theoretical

I've co-founded a business, managed client relationships across Finland, Pakistan, the EU, UAE and China, secured corporate sponsors for events, and helped write market entry reports that clients actually acted on. Business development advice is only useful if it connects to execution — and I've done the execution.

I work best with companies that have a product or service that's working in one market and want to expand — whether geographically, into new customer segments, or through partnership channels they haven't explored yet.

What I do

01
Market entry strategy
Opportunity sizing, competitive landscape, regulatory considerations, pricing strategy, and a phased entry plan with realistic milestones and resource requirements.
02
Partnership development
Identifying the right partners, building outreach that gets responses, structuring the initial conversation, and drafting partnership terms that protect both sides.
03
Revenue diversification
Mapping adjacent revenue streams — new products, new segments, new channels — against your current capabilities and deciding which are worth pursuing first.
04
Execution planning
A concrete 90-day action plan with owners, timelines, and dependencies — turning strategic decisions into operational steps your team can actually run.

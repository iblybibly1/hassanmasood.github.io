Measurable from the start

Good marketing isn't creative guesswork — it's a structured process of hypothesis, measurement, and iteration. I start every engagement by defining what success looks like in numbers, then build the tracking infrastructure to see it in real time.

I've run these frameworks in practice: audience segmentation analysis that reshaped a product line strategy, KPI dashboards that replaced two days of manual reporting per quarter, and campaign reviews that surfaced where budget was being wasted.

What I do

01
Audience & market research
Segmentation analysis, competitor review, TAM sizing, customer interview frameworks. Understanding who you're talking to before spending a euro on reaching them.
02
Channel strategy
Channel mix recommendation based on your audience, budget, and goals — organic search, email, paid social, content — with a realistic timeline and expected returns.
03
KPI dashboards
Power BI or GA4 dashboards that give you a clear view of what's working each week — traffic, conversions, revenue, cost per acquisition — without needing to build a spreadsheet every month.
04
Monthly review & optimisation
Structured monthly reviews covering what performed, what didn't, what changes for next month, and why. Not just a numbers summary — a decision framework.
